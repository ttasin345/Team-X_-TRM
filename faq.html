1. Which countries do you help students apply to?
We provide guidance for study destinations including Australia, Canada, USA, UK, Germany, and other European countries. Our counselors help you choose the best country based on your academic background, career goals, and budget.
2. Do I need IELTS/TOEFL/PTE to study abroad?
Most universities in English-speaking countries require proof of English proficiency, such as IELTS, TOEFL, or PTE. However, some universities in Europe and even in the UK or USA offer exemptions if you studied in an English-medium school or meet other criteria.
3. What is the process for applying to study abroad?
The process generally includes:
- Career counseling & country/course selection
- Preparing and submitting applications to universities
- Receiving an offer letter
- Paying tuition deposit
- Applying for a student visa
- Pre-departure briefing and travel arrangements

5. Can I work while studying abroad?
Yes, most countries allow international students to work part-time:
- Australia: Up to 48 hours per fortnight
- Canada: Up to 20 hours/week during term time
- UK: Up to 20 hours/week during term time
- USA: On-campus work only (20 hours/week) unless you have special authorization (CPT/OPT)
- Germany: 120 full days or 240 half days per year
